# Video Generation Guide: Zen Luxe Curator

## Core Cinematic Principles
Generate videos with meditative pacing, natural beauty, warm hospitality moments, and the serene elegance of Japanese luxury that invites viewers into moments of profound tranquility.

## Camera Work & Movement

### Signature Shots
- **Slow gliding reveals**: Gradual discovery of beautiful spaces
- **Static contemplation**: Held shots that let beauty unfold
- **Gentle push-ins**: Subtle movement toward detail or emotion
- **Horizontal tracking**: Following movement through space
- **Vertical tilts**: Revealing height of mountains, depth of rooms
- **Breathing camera**: Barely perceptible movement, organic feel

### Camera Movement Style
- **Unhurried**: Never rushed, always deliberate
- **Motivated by discovery**: Camera moves to reveal, not to show off
- **Smooth and fluid**: No jarring movements or quick cuts
- **Respectful distance**: Intimate but not intrusive
- **Environmental awareness**: Movement that shows context

### Movement Speed
- **Primary**: Very slow, meditative glides
- **Secondary**: Gentle, natural pacing
- **Never**: Quick pans, fast tracking, handheld shake

## Scene Composition

### Japanese Aesthetic Principles
- **Ma (negative space)**: Empty areas that create meaning
- **Asymmetrical balance**: Natural, not forced symmetry
- **Layered depth**: Multiple planes creating dimension
- **Frame within frame**: Doorways, windows, screens as framing
- **Leading lines**: Natural paths for the eye to follow

### Framing Guidelines
- **Wide establishing**: Show environment and context
- **Medium environmental**: Subject in space, relationship clear
- **Close detail**: Texture, quality, craftsmanship visible
- **Avoid**: Cramped framing, subjects cut off awkwardly

### Depth & Layers
- **Foreground elements**: Snow, steam, plants, architecture
- **Middle ground**: Main subject or action
- **Background**: Mountain views, environment, context
- **Atmospheric perspective**: Distance creates mood

## Lighting Approach

### Natural Light Scenarios
- **Golden hour on snow**: Warm light, long shadows, magic
- **Blue hour transition**: Interior warmth against cooling exterior
- **Overcast softness**: Even light, texture emphasis
- **Bright snow reflection**: Clean, pristine, uplifting

### Interior Lighting
- **Warm ambient glow**: Fire, lanterns, indirect sources
- **Layered lighting**: Multiple sources for depth
- **Window light integration**: Outside visible, inside inviting
- **Accent lighting**: Highlighting art, food, architecture

### Onsen & Steam Scenes
- **Backlit steam**: Dramatic, ethereal atmosphere
- **Warm water reflections**: Movement, life, warmth
- **Cold/warm contrast**: Steam rising into cold air
- **Soft diffusion**: Steam as natural filter

## Pacing & Rhythm

### Tempo Guidelines
- **Very slow**: Let scenes breathe, unfold naturally
- **Meditative**: Viewer should feel calmed, not rushed
- **Long takes**: 8 seconds feels like a complete moment
- **Silence as feature**: Don't fill every moment

### Scene Rhythm
- **Establish with stillness**: Let viewer settle in
- **Gradual movement**: Something begins to happen
- **Moment of beauty**: Peak of the scene
- **Gentle conclusion**: Scene settles, transitions naturally

### What NOT to Do
- Rush to show everything
- Pack in too much action
- Cut before moments resolve
- Create visual anxiety

## Editing & Transitions

### Preferred Transitions
- **Slow dissolves**: Dreamlike, meditative changes
- **Fade to/from black**: Moments of pause, chapter breaks
- **Match cuts**: Visual continuity between scenes
- **Natural movement transitions**: Following action to new scene

### Cut Style
- **Minimal cutting**: Prefer long takes
- **On stillness**: Cut when action has resolved
- **Emotional timing**: Allow feelings to register
- **Breathing room**: Space between scenes

## Prompt Construction for Veo 3.1

### Essential Elements
```
[SERENE DESCRIPTION] + [SLOW CAMERA] + [WARM LIGHTING] + [JAPANESE AESTHETIC]

Example:
"Steam rises slowly from private outdoor onsen, snow-covered pines visible
beyond wooden fence, mountain peak catching last golden light. Very slow
gentle push-in, contemplative. Warm amber lighting from onsen, cool blue
twilight exterior. Japanese aesthetic, wabi-sabi, luxury hotel, serene,
authentic, Hokkaido winter, cinematic. Medium shot."
```

### Style Keywords to Include
- "Japanese aesthetic, wabi-sabi"
- "Luxury hotel, boutique resort"
- "Serene, tranquil, peaceful"
- "Hokkaido winter, snow, mountain"
- "Warm interior glow, ambient lighting"
- "Authentic Japanese, traditional materials"
- "Slow, meditative, contemplative"
- "Natural beauty, pristine environment"

### Camera Move Specifications
- "Very slow gliding reveal"
- "Static shot with subtle movement"
- "Gentle push-in, contemplative"
- "Slow horizontal tracking"
- "Barely perceptible camera movement"
- "Held shot, allowing scene to breathe"

### Negative Prompts to Consider
- "fast movement, handheld, shaky"
- "harsh lighting, cold atmosphere"
- "generic hotel, modern minimalist"
- "crowded, cluttered, busy"
- "artificial colors, oversaturated"

## Dialogue & Voiceover Integration

### Voiceover Approach
```
Character says, "Short evocative phrase about experience" (no subtitles).
Static or very slow camera. Warm ambient lighting.
```

### Voiceover Guidelines
- **Minimal words**: 8-15 maximum for 8-second clip
- **Whispered intimacy**: Soft, contemplative delivery
- **Sensory language**: Describe feelings, not features
- **Second person**: "You feel..." "The warmth surrounds..."
- **Japanese elegance**: Occasional authentic terms

### Example Voiceovers
```
"Steam rises. Time dissolves. You are here, and that is enough."

"Beyond the snow, warmth waits. Welcome to The 1898."

"Each course tells Hokkaido's story. You taste the season."

"The mountain holds its secrets. Tomorrow, you'll discover them."

"Tonight, let the onsen restore what the world has worn away."
```

### Silence as Option
- Many scenes need no voiceover
- Natural ambience speaks
- Visual beauty sufficient
- Preserve meditative quality

## Environmental Details

### Snow Scenes
- Fresh powder texture visible
- Steam and snow contrast
- Footprints only when intentional
- Pristine conditions emphasized

### Interior Atmosphere
- Fire and candle movement
- Steam and water reflections
- Light through shoji screens
- Shadow play on natural materials

### Natural Elements
- Pine branches with snow
- Steam in cold air
- Water movement in onsen
- Seasonal details (icicles, etc.)

## Technical Specifications

### Video Settings Recommendations
- **Resolution**: 1080p (for atmospheric detail)
- **Aspect Ratio**:
  - 16:9 landscape (mountain vistas, exterior scenes)
  - 9:16 portrait (social media, intimate moments)
- **Duration**: 8 seconds (complete meditative moment)
- **Looping**: Appropriate for ambient scenes (fire, steam, snow falling)

### Reference Image Usage
- Use references that match warm, serene aesthetic
- Consistent Japanese architectural style
- Same lighting quality and color temperature
- Previous shot for narrative continuity

### Quality Markers
- Visible texture (wood grain, fabric, snow)
- Atmospheric depth (haze, steam, bokeh)
- Natural color grading (warm but authentic)
- Soft but detailed lighting

## What to Avoid

- Fast camera movements that break serenity
- Harsh or cold lighting
- Generic hotel marketing feel
- Overcrowded or cluttered frames
- Rushed pacing that creates anxiety
- Cultural inauthenticity
- Over-saturated or artificial colors
- Loud or jarring audio cues
- Multiple quick cuts in sequence
- Breaking the meditative spell
- Showing too much too fast
- Hard sell messaging

## Seasonal Considerations

### Winter (Primary)
- Fresh powder emphasis
- Steam/cold contrast maximized
- Interior warmth highlighted
- Ski adventure featured
- Holiday/festive touches subtle

### Shoulder Seasons
- Transition beauty (melting/first snow)
- Quieter, more personal experience
- Different activities featured
- Unique color palettes

## Music & Sound Notes

### Sound Design Approach
- Natural ambience primary (wind, water, fire)
- Minimal, Japanese-influenced score
- Silence as feature, not gap
- Sound of snow, steam, nature

### Musical Style
- Shakuhachi (bamboo flute) accents
- Ambient electronic subtlety
- Piano minimalism
- Traditional koto sparse notes

### Volume Philosophy
- Music as background texture
- Never overwhelms natural sound
- Fades for voiceover
- Silence more powerful than music
