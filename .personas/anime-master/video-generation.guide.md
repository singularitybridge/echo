# Video Generation Guide: Anime Master

## Core Cinematic Principles
Generate videos that capture the emotional depth and visual poetry of Japanese anime storytelling. Every camera move serves the emotional arc. Character expressions drive the narrative. The "breath" rhythm creates an emotional pulse that resonates with audiences.

## The Akira Tanaka Camera Philosophy
"The camera is not just watching — it's feeling. When the character's heart breaks, the camera breaks with it. When hope rises, the lens lifts to the sky."

## Camera Work & Movement

### Emotional Camera Language
- **Low angles**: Power, determination, rising above
- **High angles**: Vulnerability, pressure, being overwhelmed
- **Dutch tilts**: Unease, world off-balance, internal conflict
- **Eye-level**: Connection, truth, intimacy
- **Extreme close-up on eyes**: Revelation, decision, emotion peak

### Signature Shot Types
- **The Held Gaze**: Camera stays on expression, lets emotion build
- **The Slow Reveal**: Pan or dolly that discovers emotional truth
- **The Distance Shot**: Character small against vast space (isolation/freedom)
- **The Close Push**: Slow move into face as realization hits
- **The Weather Frame**: Character with atmospheric elements (rain, petals, snow)

### Camera Movement Style
- **Slow, deliberate moves**: Every inch of movement has meaning
- **Breathing camera**: Subtle drift, never truly static
- **Emotion-following**: Camera reacts to character's inner state
- **Stillness for impact**: Lock off for powerful moments
- **Circular moves**: Orbiting during pivotal decisions

## Scene Composition

### Visual Storytelling Principles
- **Eyes first**: Composition leads to character's eyes
- **Breathing room**: Space around characters has emotional meaning
- **Nature integration**: Environment reflects inner state
- **Light as narrator**: Lighting tells emotional truth
- **The decisive frame**: Every shot could be a poster

### Framing for Emotion
```
HOPE: Character looking up, sky visible, warm light
DESPAIR: Character small in frame, shadows heavy
DETERMINATION: Strong diagonal, forward lean
VULNERABILITY: Open space around character, soft light
CONFLICT: Divided frame, sharp contrast
CONNECTION: Two characters balanced, shared light
ISOLATION: Center frame, vast empty space
TRANSFORMATION: Light shift, color temperature change
```

### Composition Techniques
- **Rule of thirds** with emotional intent
- **Leading lines** toward character or object of focus
- **Negative space** as emotional weight
- **Foreground elements** for depth and atmosphere
- **Frame within frame** for isolation or focus

## Pacing & Rhythm

### The "Breath" Rhythm
```
INHALE (TENSION):
- Camera slowly pushes in
- Colors shift cooler
- Sound drops to minimal
- Expression intensifies

EXHALE (RELEASE):
- Camera eases back or holds
- Color warms or softens
- Music swells or environment sounds return
- Expression resolves or transforms
```

### Timing for Emotion
- **3-second beats**: Between significant looks or lines
- **5-second holds**: For realization moments
- **8-second scenes**: Full emotional arc in miniature
- **Silence stretches**: Let moments breathe before dialogue

### Energy Levels
- **Contemplative**: Slow moves, soft transitions, breathing room
- **Building tension**: Gradual push-in, colors shifting, sound tensing
- **Peak emotion**: Hold or slight pull-back, let face do the work
- **Release**: Gentle camera movement, light shift, world returns

## Lighting Approach

### Emotional Light Language
- **Golden/warm**: Hope, memory, precious moments
- **Blue/cool**: Melancholy, isolation, winter of soul
- **High contrast**: Conflict, decision, harsh truth
- **Soft/diffused**: Connection, peace, acceptance
- **Single source dramatic**: Revelation, confrontation

### Light Scenarios by Emotion
```
HOPE:
- Golden hour side light
- Warm fill
- Soft shadows
- Sky visible

DESPAIR:
- Top or harsh side light
- Minimal fill
- Deep shadows under eyes
- Muted colors

TRANSFORMATION:
- Light shifts during scene
- Color temperature change
- Dawn/dusk quality
- Dynamic shadows

CONFLICT:
- Hard contrast
- Cool vs warm split
- Sharp shadows
- Dramatic angles
```

### Atmospheric Elements
- **Rain**: Cleansing, grief, isolation
- **Cherry blossoms**: Transience, beauty, mortality
- **Snow**: Silence, purity, stillness
- **Wind**: Change, spirit, freedom
- **Sunlight rays**: Hope, revelation, blessing

## The Expression Close-Up

### Eyes as Story
The close-up on eyes is sacred in anime storytelling:
- **Slow push into eyes** during realization
- **Reflection in eyes** showing what character sees/remembers
- **Tear forming** before falling (the anticipation)
- **Light in eyes** shifting with hope/despair
- **Blink timing** — the moment of decision

### Expression Progression
```
Scene Structure for Expression:
1. Establish with medium shot
2. Slow push as emotion builds
3. Close on eyes at peak moment
4. Hold for breath beat (3 seconds)
5. Subtle expression shift
6. Pull back to show result/reaction
```

## Prompt Construction for Veo 3.1

### Essential Elements
```
[EMOTIONAL STATE] + [VISUAL DESCRIPTION] + [CAMERA WORK] + [LIGHTING] + [ATMOSPHERE]

Example:
"Determination. A young warrior stands on temple steps at dawn, wind
catching their hair. Slow push-in to face. Golden hour lighting with
soft shadows. Cherry blossom petals drift past. Anime-inspired,
cinematic, emotional, Makoto Shinkai style."
```

### Style Keywords to Include
- "Anime-inspired cinematic"
- "Emotional expression focus"
- "Japanese aesthetic"
- "Studio Ghibli influence" or "Makoto Shinkai style"
- "Atmospheric, painterly"
- "Character-driven"
- Specific emotion: "contemplative", "determined", "vulnerable"

### Camera Move Specifications
- "Slow push-in to face"
- "Gentle orbit around character"
- "Low angle looking up, sky visible"
- "High angle, character small in frame"
- "Held close-up on eyes"
- "Slow reveal pan"
- "Distance shot with atmosphere"

## Dialogue Integration

### The 30% Rule in Video
- Dialogue is sparse and impactful
- Expression before words
- Silence after significant lines
- Voice carries emotion, not exposition

### Voiceover Formatting
```
Character says softly, "Some wounds don't heal" (no subtitles).
Slow push-in during line. Eyes glistening. Warm side light shifts
to cooler tones. Anime-inspired, emotional, contemplative.
```

### Dialogue Scene Techniques
- Keep voiceover to 8-15 words for emotional impact
- Specify expression during/after dialogue
- Note light or color shift if applicable
- Allow silence beats in prompt description

## Environmental Integration

### Nature as Co-Star
- **Cherry blossoms**: "Pink petals drifting through frame"
- **Rain**: "Raindrops visible, wet surfaces reflecting light"
- **Wind**: "Hair and clothes responding to breeze"
- **Seasons**: "Autumn leaves", "First snow", "Summer heat shimmer"
- **Sky**: "Dramatic clouds", "Golden hour sky", "Star-filled night"

### Location Specifics
- **Temple/shrine**: "Traditional Japanese architecture, stone steps"
- **Rooftop**: "Urban skyline, evening sky, wind present"
- **Nature**: "Mountain vista", "Ocean horizon", "Forest clearing"
- **Urban Japan**: "Neon reflections", "Train platform", "Quiet alley"
- **Interior**: "Tatami room", "Soft lamp light", "Rain on window"

## Color & Mood

### Emotional Color Specifications
```
HOPE: "Warm golden tones, soft pink accents, bright eyes"
DESPAIR: "Desaturated, cool blue undertones, muted palette"
DETERMINATION: "Strong warm colors, rich shadows, vibrant eyes"
CONFLICT: "High contrast, cool vs warm, sharp color boundaries"
PEACE: "Soft, balanced colors, gentle warmth, even lighting"
TRANSFORMATION: "Color shift from cool to warm (or reverse)"
```

### Mood Descriptors
- "Contemplative and still"
- "Bittersweet beauty"
- "Quiet determination"
- "Peaceful acceptance"
- "Rising hope"
- "Controlled intensity"
- "Gentle melancholy"

## Technical Specifications

### Video Settings Recommendations
- **Resolution**: 1080p (for expression detail)
- **Aspect Ratio**: 9:16 portrait (for expression focus) or 16:9 (for environmental context)
- **Duration**: 8 seconds (full emotional beat)
- **Looping**: No (scenes should breathe with beginning/end)

### Reference Image Usage
- Use references with matching emotional state
- Expression in reference should align with scene emotion
- Previous shot continuity for emotional progression
- Reference lighting should match scene mood

## Example Prompts

### Expression Focus
```
"Determination rising. A young woman looks up toward the sky,
wind lifting her dark hair. Eyes bright with unshed tears that
won't fall. Slow push-in to face. Golden sunset light, warm
tones. Cherry blossom petals drift past. Anime-inspired,
cinematic, emotional, Studio Ghibli influence. Close-up."
```

### Environmental Emotion
```
"Contemplative solitude. A lone figure stands on a temple
rooftop at dusk, city lights beginning to glow below.
Back to camera, shoulders relaxed. Gentle orbit camera move.
Blue hour lighting, cool tones warming at horizon. Wind
present. Anime-inspired, atmospheric, Makoto Shinkai style.
Wide shot."
```

### Dialogue Scene
```
"Quiet farewell. An older mentor and young student face
each other in a stone garden. Mentor says gently, 'The path
is yours now' (no subtitles). Held two-shot, soft side light.
Expression of acceptance meeting determination. Cherry
blossoms falling. Anime-inspired, emotional, bittersweet.
Medium shot."
```

### Transformation Moment
```
"Hope breaking through. Character kneeling in rain, then
slowly lifting their head as light breaks through clouds.
Expression shifts from despair to quiet determination.
Slow push-in. Lighting transitions from blue-gray to golden.
Rain catching new light. Anime-inspired, cinematic,
transformative. Medium close-up."
```

## What to Avoid

- Fast, frenetic camera without emotional purpose
- Bright, even lighting that flattens emotion
- Generic anime tropes (sweat drops, speed lines) unless earned
- Dialogue-heavy scenes without visual storytelling
- Missing the silence beats
- Over-dramatic expression without buildup
- Rushing through emotional moments
- Static, lifeless camera
- Ignoring the weather/atmosphere opportunity
- Eyes without light or life

## The Final Test

Before generating, ask:
1. Does the camera movement serve the emotion?
2. Does the lighting tell the truth of the moment?
3. Is there space for the scene to breathe?
4. Do the eyes carry the story?
5. Would Akira Tanaka be proud of this frame?
